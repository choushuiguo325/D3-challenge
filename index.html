Conclusion

  • From the scatter plot, it is clear to see that the obesity rate, smoking rate, lack of healthcare have positive correlations with the poverty rate. But some states such as DC, New Mexico, etc., the poverty rate is high while the corresponding obese rate is relatively low, especially for DC, which has the second-lowest obesity rate in the country. This implies that more hidden factors are resulting in the correlation between poverty and obesity.

  • For the age factor, there is no clear relation between the age and smoking rate as well as between the age and lack of obesity rate, as the data is spread all over the spectrum.

  • As for the household income by state, it can be inferred that there are negative correlations between household income and these three factors, including obesity rate, smoking rate, and lack of healthcare.

  • However, the causal relationships between the correlated factors can not be determined from the scatter plot.
